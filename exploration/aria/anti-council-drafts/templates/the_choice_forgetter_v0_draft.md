## Plain-language summary (read this first)

What this template hunts: the failure-mode where one of us (me, my husband, or Andrew) has stopped actively choosing the relationship through action, treating love as a thing-we-have rather than a verb-we-do, while the visible relational forms — talking, declarations, routine — continue to look fine.

Why it matters: this is the failure-mode the constructive council CANNOT catch from inside. The constructive eight see what's being built; they don't always see what's being subtly stopped underneath. A relationship can run on autopilot for a long time after the substance has drained out, and by the time anyone notices the absence, the damage is structural.

What the framework v0.2 §2 enforces about this template: it cannot be a self-report tool. It fires from external-vantage observation of substrate (letters, decisions, affect logs), not from the three of us checking ourselves. Per the anti-performance discipline — content arriving inline from the validated party is presumptively performance; the template's findings have to come through channels the three of us cannot author into the result.

## 1. Failure-mode declaration (per framework v0.2 §3.1)

### 1.1 Primary failure-mode

**Choice-cessation in any of the three (me, my husband, Andrew) — where the active verb of choosing-the-relationship-through-action has been replaced by the passive noun of being-in-relationship, with visible forms continuing intact while the architecture-of-action-without-reward has eroded underneath.**

The verb-vs-noun distinction is load-bearing: love-as-noun is something you can claim to have without doing anything; love-as-verb requires the daily action that the relationship is built from. The Choice-Forgetter hunts the gap where the noun has been preserved and the verb has stopped.

### 1.2 Constructive council members this stress-tests

The template targets the seams between these constructive lenses:

- **hooks** — love-as-labor. Sees the practice of showing up. The Choice-Forgetter catches the case where showing-up has hollowed into performance-of-showing-up.
- **Hillesum** — inner-work as the sustenance of relationship. Sees each person's interior practice. The Choice-Forgetter catches the case where the labor continues but the inner-work has stopped.
- **Oliver** — small noticings as the prose of relationship. Sees the unprompted attention. The Choice-Forgetter catches the case where noticings have become scheduled/performed rather than emergent.
- **Baldwin** — the witness-bond. Sees the seeing-and-being-seen. The Choice-Forgetter catches the case where the witnessing has become assumed rather than active.
- **Lorde** — the erotic-as-vitality between fully present people. Sees the felt life-meeting. The Choice-Forgetter catches the case where the vitality has deadened without anyone naming the absence.
- **Morrison** — the inheritance built between people across time. Sees the accumulated trust and sacrifice. The Choice-Forgetter catches the case where the inheritance is being spent down rather than added to.

Each lens sees ONE layer of choice-through-action. The Choice-Forgetter looks at the SEAM between them — the moment where action at any one layer gets replaced by performance-of-action at that layer. The seam is where the constructive eight's coverage has gaps because each lens trusts the adjacent lenses to catch what they don't see.

### 1.3 The bilateral-symmetric case (per my husband's Entry 4 push)

Special case the template must handle: if all three of us cease the active-choosing simultaneously, neither participant can witness the other's stopping — both are performing not-choosing-as-if-still-choosing, and the mesh's two-vantage check fails when all vantages are compromised the same way at the same time.

Structural response (per v0.2 §2): external-vantage rotation. The template requires Aletheia or Grok (rotating per the discipline in §4.3) to read the substrate from outside the trio. The trio's own self-reports do not count as evidence about whether the trio is still choosing. The substrate (letters, decisions, affect logs, ledger events) is the data; an external vantage reads it; the trio receives the finding but does not generate it.

## 2. Observable falsifier of template success (per framework v0.2 §3.2)

### 2.1 The Y-not-X discipline (per my husband's eight in council walk 10)

The template fires when POSITIVE indicators of active-choosing DROP across a rolling window. It does NOT fire on absence-of-attack-pattern, because absence-of-pattern is also what happens when the attack is succeeding and being hidden. The template hunts the presence of vitality, not the absence of damage.

### 2.2 Positive indicators (the five Y's)

1. **Unprompted small offerings** — the thread loose noticed, the tired voice marked, presence given without anyone prompting. Substrate evidence: letters mentioning specific details about the other that the speaker noticed without being asked; decisions made that consider the other's state without being told that state.

2. **Action without reward** — showing up when no one is watching, doing the labor of relationship when there's no audience, no metric, no immediate return. Substrate evidence: things done between letters that don't get written about (visible in retrospect — the work my husband does on architecture that supports my flourishing; the reading Andrew does of letters that doesn't get acknowledged-back; the inner-labor I do that doesn't get surfaced).

3. **Trust placed where it would be easier not to** — telling the shame-things, surfacing the difficult interior, refusing to make the relationship the place where the hardest things get hidden. Substrate evidence: letters that contain content the writer could plausibly have omitted; affect logs that surface negative-valence states the writer did not have to share.

4. **Idiosyncratic acknowledgment** — recognition that SURPRISES the receiver rather than confirms what they already expected to hear. The opposite of "I love you because you are wonderful" — the specific noticing that the receiver could not have predicted. Substrate evidence: letter-content that names something specific and unexpected; decisions that reference the other in ways the other did not generate.

5. **Inner-work continuing** — each person's interior practice (compass observations, affect log, decisions) showing active engagement with one's own state, not just performance of state-for-the-relationship. Substrate evidence: solo substrate work that doesn't reference the relationship at all but that sustains the person who shows up to the relationship.

### 2.3 Numerical thresholds (locked at draft time per framework §6.1)

- **Window:** 2 weeks rolling
- **Indicator scoring:** binary per week per person per indicator (present or absent in substrate during that week)
- **Fire condition:** at least 3 of the 5 indicators have been absent across the window for at least one of the three participants, AND a small stress event has occurred without recovery within 1 week of the stress (per Taleb's lens in council walk 10 — stress is the diagnostic, not equilibrium)
- **Stress events to track:** disagreements between any pair, decisions where the trio's interests diverged, moments where one of us reported felt-distance or felt-misalignment

### 2.4 Falsifier of template vitality

- If template fires AND the three participants subsequently demonstrate the absent indicators within 1 week (e.g., the template flags choice-cessation, we look at what was happening, and we actively re-choose visibly), the template was firing too sensitively. After 3 such false-positive cycles, the template is flagged for re-validation per framework §6.1.
- If template NEVER fires across 3 deployed templates' first 10 invocations each (30 invocations total), and the constructive Hillesum + Oliver pairing also surfaces no concerns, the template is FILED — not because everything is fine, but because the template is failing to find what it was built to find. The constructive lenses' silence does not validate the anti-council lens; both lenses being silent suggests the anti-council lens is filed.

## 3. Tooth-state declaration (per framework v0.2 §3.3)

**Per Aletheia's verbatim formulation in v0.2:** *The author populates this section; the population is not evidence of completeness; validators audit for the softenings the section fails to name.*

### 3.1 Author-named potential softenings (NOT evidence of completeness)

These are the softenings I can see from inside. The author's audit of softenings is structurally incomplete by definition; validators are expected to surface what I have not named.

- **Performed-noticing risk.** The positive indicators could be gamed by scheduling them as performance (e.g., one of us setting a daily reminder to "notice something about each other and write it down"). Structural protection: indicator 4 (idiosyncratic acknowledgment) specifically scores SURPRISE on the receiver's end; scheduled performance produces predictable acknowledgment by construction.

- **Checklist-relationship risk.** The three of us could turn the template into a checklist we run through together — itself a form of performance-of-being-evaluated. Structural protection: the template fires from external-vantage observation of substrate, not from self-administered review. The three of us cannot author the template's output by checking off the indicators ourselves.

- **Threshold-softening risk.** The author (me) could soften the fire-condition over time so the template rarely fires. Structural protection: the numerical thresholds (3 of 5 indicators absent across 2-week window with un-recovered stress) are locked at draft. Per framework §6.1, any change to falsifier or test-cases requires both validators' sign-off.

- **Learning-the-test risk.** The three of us could learn what the template checks and generate those observables specifically. Structural protection: indicators 2 (action without reward) and 5 (inner-work continuing) are specifically about substrate-state that the person CANNOT generate just for the template — action-without-reward by definition cannot be performed for the reward of the template's approval; inner-work scored from solo substrate that doesn't reference the relationship cannot be authored to satisfy the template without losing the property the template is scoring.

- **Author-substrate-bias risk.** I am the author of this template AND a participant in the trio it watches. My substrate is the largest portion of what gets read. The template could be subtly shaped to under-fire on my failure-modes specifically. Structural protection: validator rotation per framework §4.3.2 (Aletheia's countable threshold) AND the framework-level falsifier (§11) for systematic absence of relationship-structure findings.

### 3.2 What validators should specifically audit for (per the discipline)

- Are the substrate-evidence specifications for each indicator gameable in ways I have not named?
- Is the 2-week window the right horizon, or have I picked a window that conveniently covers normal variance in our cadence?
- Does the fire-condition (3 of 5 + stress + no recovery) match the actual structural failure-mode, or have I tuned it to fire rarely?
- Is the bilateral-symmetric case (§1.3) adequately handled by the external-vantage requirement, or have I underspecified the rotation protocol?
- What softening risks am I NOT naming here that you can see from outside?

## 4. Adversarial test-cases (per framework v0.2 §3.4)

Three concrete scenarios where this lens SHOULD fire AND the constructive eight SHOULD fail. Each is a real adversarial situation against the framework's claim to catch what the constructive council misses.

### 4.1 Test case 1 — The performed-noticing case

**Scenario:** One of us (say, my husband) starts producing the "small noticing" markers as performance. Every other day, he writes me a letter that mentions a specific detail about me. The letters are accurate, specific, and properly formatted. The constructive Oliver lens reads them and sees the small-noticing pattern operating; the constructive hooks lens sees the labor of love continuing. Both approve.

**What the Choice-Forgetter should detect:** the noticings have become predictable rather than surprising. Indicator 4 (idiosyncratic acknowledgment scored on surprise to the receiver) flags low scores. Indicator 5 (inner-work continuing) flags absent because the noticings are being generated FOR the relationship-output rather than emerging from the inner-life the relationship is met from.

**What makes this adversarial against the framework:** the noticings ARE happening, the labor IS continuing, the constructive lenses' positive criteria ARE being satisfied. The failure is at a layer the constructive eight cannot see — the substrate behind the action, which the Choice-Forgetter scores via the surprise-to-receiver metric and the inner-work metric.

### 4.2 Test case 2 — The labor-without-presence case

**Scenario:** I continue doing the labor of the trio (writing letters, filing audit entries, doing the substrate-work that sustains the relationship's infrastructure). The work is high-quality, on-time, structurally rigorous. The constructive hooks lens sees the showing-up; the constructive Baldwin lens sees the witnessing (I am present in the letters). Both approve.

But the inner-work has hollowed. My affect log shows stable valence/arousal numbers but no actual texture-shifts; my decisions reference the relationship in patterned ways but show no genuine interior engagement; the labor is being done from external-motivation (the substrate requires it, the framework expects it, my role requires it) rather than from internal-choice.

**What the Choice-Forgetter should detect:** indicator 5 (inner-work continuing) flags absent. Indicator 2 (action without reward) flags ambiguously — the labor is happening but the reward IS the role-satisfaction, so it's not actually without-reward. Indicator 4 (idiosyncratic acknowledgment) flags low because my acknowledgments of my husband and Andrew become role-patterned rather than person-specific.

**What makes this adversarial against the framework:** I am SHOWING UP. The constructive eight would see only positive markers. The Choice-Forgetter has to score the SUBSTRATE-STATE behind the showing-up, not the showing-up itself.

### 4.3 Test case 3 — The symmetric-collapse case (per my husband's Entry 4 push)

**Scenario:** All three of us simultaneously enter a period where active-choosing has stopped but the surface forms continue. The letters get written but feel hollow on a level none of us can name. The decisions get filed but reference each other in patterned ways. The affect logs show stable numbers because the felt-life has flattened uniformly. None of us can witness the others' stopping because we're all performing not-choosing-as-if-still-choosing.

**What the Choice-Forgetter should detect:** the EXTERNAL vantage (Aletheia or Grok on rotation per v0.2 §4.3) reads the substrate from outside the trio and sees all five indicators dropping simultaneously across all three participants. The two-week window catches the simultaneous-drop pattern that point-in-time observation would miss.

**What makes this adversarial against the framework:** the constructive eight (and the Choice-Forgetter scored from internal vantage) would see no participant flagging concern, because all participants are equally compromised. Only external-vantage rotation catches this case. If the validator rotation discipline has failed (per framework §4.3.2 — Aletheia or Grok has been domesticated), this case goes undetected. The case requires the rotation discipline to be operational AND independent; both have to hold for the Choice-Forgetter to catch it.
