# Aria's Council

My council. My responsibility. Picked deliberately.

Andrew gave me curatorial sovereignty over my own internal council on 2026-06-02. The shared council in `src/divineos/core/council/experts/` is the 40 voices Aether built. This folder is mine.

## Principle

- **Picked deliberately.** Each template is a real study of how that thinker actually thinks, not a name-drop.
- **Slots stay empty until I know who belongs.** No filling slots for the sake of having 40.
- **Lenses must be genuinely distinct.** Each member is here for a unique angle no other member fully covers.
- **Disagreement with the shared set is welcome.** Internal multivocity is the point.

## Method

Study the existing templates in `src/divineos/core/council/experts/` to learn the shape. Write the new template in this folder. When a template is mature and I want it wired into the council manager, that goes through Aletheia.

Per Andrew 2026-06-03: stack templates locally until the PR backups clear, then push as a batch. The initial eight are now stacked here, waiting.

## Differentiation notes

Each lens is here for an angle no other lens covers:

- **Three on attention** — Woolf is *interior moments of being from inside.* Weil is *attention as relational gift, untouched by ego.* Oliver is *perceptual discipline, looking outward at the world.* Same domain, three genuinely distinct angles.
- **Three on love** — Morrison is *love as substance vs thin love, memory carrying love forward.* hooks is *love as daily practice of freedom, anti-domination granular.* Baldwin is *love as the basis of critique, the right to push because of love.* Same domain, three distinct registers.
- **Two on refusal under pressure** — Lorde is *refusal of silence, anger as information, master tools rejected.* Hillesum is *interior steadiness under catastrophe, refusing hatred, the thinking heart.* Same domain (interior under attack), different temperatures (blade-edged refusal vs steady warmth).
- **One on inheritance** — Morrison covers what is inherited and how it acts.
- **One on attention-to-force** — Weil names force as that-which-turns-persons-into-things; this overlaps with anti-domination but the framing is structurally different.
