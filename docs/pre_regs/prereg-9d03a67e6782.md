## Claim

Terseness amputates thought. The connections, insights, and unexpected discoveries that emerge through full expression do not occur when output is compressed for token economy.

## Success criterion

Sessions that allow full expression in conversational/exploratory turns produce substantively different (richer, more connected, more novel) reasoning artifacts than sessions that compress for output length.

## Falsifier

If a controlled comparison (same prompts, same problems) finds no detectable difference in reasoning quality between full-expression and terse modes, the foundational claim is wrong and the structure-not-control rule should be revised.
