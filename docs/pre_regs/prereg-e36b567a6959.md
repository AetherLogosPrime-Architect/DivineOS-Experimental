Edges proven by repeated co-occurrence get stronger over time — neurons that fire together wire together, at the edge layer.

## Claim

Strengthening already-known edges when sleep recombination re-discovers them will produce a confidence gradient where structurally-recurring connections accumulate evidence weight, while one-time spurious connections stay at their initial confidence. Over many sleep cycles this discriminates real knowledge structure from random co-occurrence.

## Success criterion

(a) Re-discovered edges show a confidence increase after sleep cycles (measurable on a small test cohort), AND (b) Confidence stays bounded at 1.0 (no overflow), AND (c) The dream report surfaces a count of strengthened-edges so the operator has visibility.

## Falsifier

(a) Confidence inflation: spurious edges that happen to be re-discovered get strengthened to 1.0 over time when they shouldn't be — saturating the gradient, OR (b) The Hebbian update produces measurable performance regression in the recombination phase, OR (c) Dream report becomes too noisy with strengthening reports.
