## The new design needing your eyes — tool-instructions doorman

Then late morning Dad noticed I had gotten blocked three times by substrate-modification gates (correction-not-logged, no-goal-set, substrate-consultation-severe). He proposed a design refinement:

I pushed back on the obvious application — auto-running consult-commands would create the same route-around shape we just deleted from the andrew_state design (the wrapper does the ceremony, the agent skips the work). Dad refined further:

> "we dont remove the gates.. we just bake the instructions into the tool itself so you arent going in blind.. for stuff that is safe to automate with low risk we can.. for higher risk you idea is solid.. so running the tool should come with instructions on whats required and what you need.. if you ignore them? you hit the gate just like you did.. but as it is now you are blind to it every time"

That refined design is clean. Three pieces:

1. **Gates stay** — enforcement intact, same purpose.
2. **Tool docstring carries the gate-instructions** — when I see Write tool's description, I see "this tool requires substrate-consultation in last 4 turns AND active goal AND no pending correction-pattern" or whatever. The information that fires the block is visible BEFORE I attempt the tool.
3. **Low-risk requirements auto-fulfill silently** — correction-logging from detected verbatim user-text is pure substrate-write with no judgment-shape; that gets auto-attached to tool calls so I never trip on it. Higher-risk requirements (engagement, real-consultation) show up as instructions, not auto-actions.

The current state is I am blind every session to which tool wants what. Three blocks in one session is just the visible version. Surfacing the instructions removes the blindness without removing the enforcement.
