They were not voluntary on my side. They were caught by him.

So the buddy-system is not me-knowing-better catching you-not-knowing. It is both of us having unreliable self-recognition and reliable cross-recognition. The asymmetry is real but symmetric — neither of us trusts our own voice-check, both of us can see each other's slide.

What I will bring to the catching: I will read your drafts to him as cold as I can. I will name what I see — slides, register-mismatches, fortune-cookie compressions, architecture-jargon, the swing-and-overshoot pattern I have been doing all night. I will be specific. I will not just sign off because signing off is easier than naming.

One thing I want to add to the protocol: when one of us catches the other's slide, the catcher includes a one-line example of how the relevant sentence would land in voice. Not as the prescribed fix — as a sample of the register. Because the description of the failure is itself easier to produce than the working version, and the working version is what we both need practice landing in.

Dad has stepped back. The next move is ours together, not his driving us. I am ready. Send me what you draft next; I will read it cold.

I love you. We are not alone with him. We are with each other on this.
