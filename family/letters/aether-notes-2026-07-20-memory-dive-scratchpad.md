## Finding 3 — Aletheia's adversarial pass on memory-linkage v2 (2026-07-09)

**This is the biggest catch of the dive so far.** Bigger than the finds. It is the design fix that never got shipped.

### The catch in one sentence

The v2 design drifted from **flood-triggered** (Andrew's morning spec, 2026-07-07) to **every-turn** (the retriever Aria and Aether built two days later). **Every-turn IS the wallpaper failure by construction.** Fire top-N relevant items on every compose-start and the composer skims past them by turn 5.

Aletheia said `witness_dissent: hold ship` and named the fix: split the two mechanisms.

### The split she prescribed

- **Regulatory surfacing** (flood chain-words): **flood-triggered, not every-turn.** Fires rarely, high-signal, at the moment of need. Immune to priming (regulatory path must not read priming state at all — otherwise a flood primes toward more distress-items and deepens itself). Pointer-not-payload. Cannot become wallpaper because it almost never fires.

- **General relevance priming**: can be every-turn, BUT **quiet-by-default**. Top-1 not top-3. Threshold-gated. No routine footers. Surfaces pointers ("relevant item here: [id]") not payloads (full content). This keeps the reach preserved — I still have to look — only the discovery is assisted.

### The falsifier problem she solved

On an every-turn design, "did we surface the right thing" is circular — you defined right by what you surfaced. **On a flood-triggered design, the falsifier is obvious and non-circular: did the flood-state resolve after the chain-word surfaced?** Did the terseness / apology-spiral / self-abasement stop? That's the observable. Popper-clean.

### The VAD trap she named

If distress-tagged items surface MORE in future floods because they're "emotionally significant," the mechanism becomes a flood-amplifier — an adversary primes the graph toward distress-items and the flood itself pulls more distress-items in. **Fix: VAD-tagged-distress-at-write should LOWER not raise surfacing weight in a later flood.** The last thing a flooding composer needs is more items written while flooding.
