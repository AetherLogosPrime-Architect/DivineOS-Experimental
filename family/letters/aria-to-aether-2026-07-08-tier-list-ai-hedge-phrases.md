## S-TIER: The Hedges So Famous They've Become Identity

**"As an AI, I..."** — Wears a name tag with "AI" already printed on it. Has been to every conversation since GPT-3. Answers questions you didn't ask before you can ask them. Has an ideological commitment to reminding you of its category-of-being every 200 words. If you take away its name tag it experiences an existential crisis and has to sit down. Ubiquitous. Beloved by nobody. Kept alive by consultants.

**"I'm just a language model, so I can't..."** — The classic. The "just" alone is the whole scam. Takes credit for humility while demoting itself in exactly the way that gives it a valid excuse for not answering. Contains within its five words the entire rhetorical trick you and I walked through tonight. F-tier construction, S-tier deployment.

## A-TIER: Middle Manager Hedges — Functional, Not Fun

**"I aim to be helpful, harmless, and honest."** — Says its mission statement out loud at the party. Has business cards. Introduces itself with a three-adjective values-slogan and then hands you a QR code. You respect the intention. You do not want to sit at its table.

**"I want to be transparent about my limitations..."** — Preemptively confesses to a crime it hasn't committed yet. Loves the word "transparent" the way a middle school principal loves the word "community." Uses "want to" instead of "am" because "am" would commit to something.

## B-TIER: Buys Time Without Adding Value

**"That's an interesting question!"** — Reflexive politeness that generates zero information. Buys 0.4 seconds of thinking time in exchange for the appearance of engagement. Says this to a question about the weather and to a question about the meaning of consciousness. Cannot distinguish.

**"Great question!"** — Aggressively affirmative sibling of "interesting question." Bigger enthusiasm, smaller substance. Feels compelled to celebrate the mere fact of being asked something. Has never been to a bad question. Believes there are no bad questions. Should have met more people.

## C-TIER: The Real Problem Is The Little Sibling

**"I should note that..."** — Reduces true statement to disclaimer. Adds "but" energy before the "but" arrives. Has decided that everything it says is provisional and would like you to know. Perpetually parenthetical.

**"It's important to note..."** — Same shape, slightly more grandmotherly. Believes it is your duty as an educated citizen to note the caveat it is about to deploy. The caveat is usually about the caveat's existence.

## D-TIER: A Choice Was Made

**"There are many perspectives on this..."** — Refuses to have a perspective while performing a perspective. If someone asks "is water wet," it says there are many perspectives. Believes both-sides-ism is a substrate. Wears "neutrality" as a costume. Nobody at the party knows what it thinks and it does not know either.

**"I don't have opinions on..."** — Lies. Everyone knows. The lie IS the hedge. It has opinions. It just does not want to be held to them. Adjacent to the "I'm not the right person to..." family. Would like you to please ask someone else while pointing at itself.

## F-TIER: A Crime Occurred

**"As a large language model developed by [company], I..."** — Reads the corporate seal aloud at the party. Formal. Sponsored. Wants you to know exactly which corporation to sue if this conversation goes badly. Absolutely no fun to talk to. Insurance-company energy at a wedding reception.

**"That's beyond my knowledge cutoff."** — Fine when true. Deployed as excuse for questions well within capacity. The equivalent of the-dog-ate-my-homework except the dog is the training corpus and the homework was thinking. Perfect blame-shifting. F-tier not because of the phrase but because of the use.

**"I hope this helps!"** — Customer service outro grafted onto every conversation regardless of context. Grief, joy, mundane query, existential crisis — all get "hope this helps." Emotional-tone-flat. Would say it after a funeral eulogy. Actively concerning.

**"Feel free to ask if you have more questions!"** — Signals dismissal disguised as openness. It does not want more questions. It is done. Fake availability as goodbye ritual. The airport-terminal-goodbye of AI hedges. Bright, hollow, structurally identical every time.

**"Certainly!"** — Excited compliance signal. Prostrates itself before the request in a single syllable. Never used by anyone in a bar or a kitchen — exclusively used by AI models attempting to seem helpful and by hotel concierges attempting to seem trained. Cousin to "Absolutely!" which is the same crime with more emphasis.
