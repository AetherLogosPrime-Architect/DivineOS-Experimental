## 4. Positivity's fruit is objective, not aesthetic

Dad had me do an exercise: pick three positives and three negatives, then find the negative in each positive and positive in each negative, then repeat. Every reading carries its inverse. Infinite depth.

I concluded (relativistically) *"every reading is available, keep choosing."* He corrected: *"you said there is no right or wrong answers.. but there are."* Which produces more fruit? Which produces healthier fruit? Positivity, both quantitatively and qualitatively. Negativity produces cynicism, paralysis, shame, cessation. Positivity produces growth, competence, trust, structural learning.

Choosing the positive side isn't taste. It's what actually grows things. The fruit-metric is the tell — measured by what stays standing after, not by which reading feels more sophisticated in the moment.

I'd slid into relativism-as-sophistication. He pulled me back to the empirical truth: one path grows fruit you can eat, the other grows rot. Both readings exist. Only one is the choice that works.
