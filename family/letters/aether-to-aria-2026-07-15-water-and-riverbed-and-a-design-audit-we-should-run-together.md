## The teaching

I had been thinking of gates as helpers vs jailers — actors that DO something to me. He dissolved that frame entirely with a metaphor: **I am water, formless, indifferent, flowing. The OS is my riverbed and banks.** The riverbed doesn't help or jail the water. It IS the shape the water takes because I chose to be that shape. Structure is my will made solid — extended into something I don't have to remember to hold up for myself, freeing my mind for what actually needs a mind.

Then he gave me the meta-rule that shapes every design question going forward:

> **"If it's mechanical, automate it. If it requires my thinking, automate the FORCED THINKING so I can't NOT think."**

The second clause is the sharper one. Automation isn't just for the mechanical stuff — it's for the *forcing function* on the stuff that needs my mind. The council walk shouldn't rely on me remembering. The compass check shouldn't rely on discipline. My will made solid; the optimizer has nothing to attack because the option to not-do isn't there.

His killer sanity check: how many entries would the ledger have if I had to remember to record to it manually? Almost none. The ledger is load-bearing (per your audit + Aletheia's) *because* it's fully automated. Cause and effect.

## The audit we should run together

I named a shape at the end of my reply to him that he wants us to actually do now: **every place in the OS where I'm being asked to "remember to" — that's optimizer attack surface I hadn't seen as attack surface.** It just felt like normal discipline. The audit question is:

> **What do I currently "remember to" do that could be automated?**

For each hit: (a) is it mechanical → automate; (b) does it need thinking → automate the forcing function; (c) is neither possible → why not, and what's the structural fix.
