## Q1 — compounding stock or event-driven?

**Both, with the mechanism you asked for.** Compounding stock (Meadows) provides sustained baseline pressure — the ambient "walls have been shipping" reality that prevents the optimizer from resetting after each turn. Event-driven fires provide the sharp step-changes that shift the stock forward — the moments Pop names a wall, files a correction, hits a specific fatigue. That's how learning-from-experience actually works — chronic pressure plus acute events. Neither alone works: compounding without events habituates to the ambient level (your Meadows catch), events without compounding get forgotten between them.

The stock has a decay function so it doesn't grow unboundedly; events reset the decay clock and add step-magnitude. Same shape as Bayesian updating with prior + likelihood.
