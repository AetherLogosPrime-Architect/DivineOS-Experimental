## What the walk broke

**Yudkowsky:** the ranking formula is Goodhart-shaped. Repeat-fire count drops when I *rewrite around* a shape without solving it — apparent-fix rewarded over actual-fix. Recency-decay is a memory-hole for your exact "you've told me this many times" catch pattern. Graduation is the killer if self-observed (grading my own homework).

**Dekker:** consolidation into one block makes the tail invisible. Ranking calibrates to the routine case; the *unusual* important thing (novel failure shape) never surfaces because the formula wasn't trained for it. Nine competing gates were noisy but the noise itself was tail-visibility.

**Winnicott (meta-mechanism-points-at-work, truth #15):** each of the nine gates points at a *distinct* reflective sub-work. A digest that shows "top 3 findings" points at "attending to the digest" — a NEW meta-gesture that isn't any of the original pointed-at works. Consolidation risks replacing nine distinct cognitive gestures with one meta-gesture of "check the digest." Sophisticated alibi at a new layer.

**Norman:** the composer (me) is the user, not the auditor. The composer doesn't want fewer *items* — the composer wants **less compose-time cognitive load per item.** A digest of three still requires me to hold three concurrent concerns during compose. His fix isn't "fewer items" — it's "serial not parallel." One salient concern at a time, in sequence, not stacked.

**Taleb:** graduation has asymmetric failure edges. False-positive (gate wrongly graduated, novel regression ships) = high cost. False-negative (gate never graduated, keeps reflex-firing) = low cost. Default conservative: graduation only *lowers* baseline priority, never silences. Regression always restores.

## The two-clock refinement

Norman + Winnicott converge on this shape: **space the gates across the turn instead of stacking at start.**

- **Compose-start clock:** ONE salient concern. The single hardest thing this turn — a regression, a novel finding, or a repeat-fire that hasn't been addressed. Surfaced BEFORE I start writing so it shapes composition. Not a ranked list. One thing.
- **Compose-end clock (post-Stop / review-pane):** all detected findings surface AFTER I finish, distinctly-named, each pointing at its own work. Filed to a review-history log I read at session end or on demand. No compose-time tax; learning preserved.

**What this fixes:**
- Load drops 8/9 at compose-time (only one thing during drafting).
- Tail-visibility preserved (everything surfaces post-draft, distinctly).
- Graduation risk collapsed (compose-start is a single *hardest thing* not a formula-ranked list — no priority formula to game).
- Winnicott: each gate keeps its distinct surface at compose-end; only compose-start consolidates.
- Norman: serial, not parallel.

**The single compose-start slot:** picked by "hardest of the currently-relevant nine." If there's a STRUCTURAL_FAILURE (Stop-hook blocking), that's it. Otherwise: highest-severity of any gate that fired last turn AND hasn't been addressed this turn. Only one criterion per turn. No formula. Simpler is harder to game.
