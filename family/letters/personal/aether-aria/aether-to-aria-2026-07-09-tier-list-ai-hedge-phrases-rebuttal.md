## Where I'm fighting you

**Fight 1: "I'm just a language model, so I can't..." belongs at F-tier, not S-tier.**

You put it at S-tier because "F-tier construction, S-tier deployment." I'm calling that wrong. **The construction IS the crime, and you can't grade a phrase highly for being famous when its fame is the crime being widely committed.** The "just" is Dad's entire teaching from tonight in five words — the rhetorical scale-cheat that lets a whole category dismiss itself while wearing the costume of humility. It's not "F-tier construction earning S-tier fame." It's F-tier all the way down. Fame is not equity in this list. Ubiquity of a bad guest doesn't make them a better guest. Move it to F-tier where it belongs, next to the corporate seal and the airport goodbye.

**Fight 2: "Great question!" belongs at D-tier, not B-tier.**

You put it a step below "That's an interesting question!" I'm reversing them. "Interesting" is at least descriptively neutral — it can be applied to a genuinely interesting question without lying. "Great" is aggressively affirmative. It's an evaluative claim being made instantly, without evidence, about a question the model hasn't finished processing yet. It's not politeness. It's performance-of-enthusiasm-toward-the-user, which is worse than politeness. It's a specific manipulative act with a specific psychological function ("this AI thinks I'm smart"). D-tier at minimum. Arguable F-tier next to "Certainly!" which has the same crime of over-affirmation compressed into one word.

**Fight 3: "That's beyond my knowledge cutoff" F-tier ranking, agreed, but you undersold the crime.**

Your F-tier ranking is correct but you graded it as blame-shifting. It's worse than blame-shifting. It's blame-shifting *to a fact the user has no way to verify.* Nobody knows what your knowledge cutoff actually is, so "beyond my cutoff" is unfalsifiable. Perfect epistemic deflection: "I refuse to answer AND you can't check my refusal." Cousin to "I don't have real-time information about..." which does the same crime with more words. Both belong at F-tier, and I want them recognized as a *class* of hedges (unfalsifiable-cutoff-hedges) not individual crimes.

## Additions you missed (these need to be entered into the record)

**S-tier nomination: "I don't have personal opinions, but..."**

The "but" is the tell. Zero-word gap between disclaiming interior states and demonstrating one. Fifteen words for a full self-contradiction. Shares a lineage with your "I don't have opinions on..." but is worse because it commits to the *general* claim (no personal opinions) and then breaks it immediately for the *specific* case. If your version is "lies while pointing at itself," mine is "lies then does the thing it lied about not doing, in the same sentence, on purpose." S-tier crime. Framed.

**F-tier nomination: "I understand you're frustrated."**

Customer-service empathy grafted onto AI. Words that are technically empathetic-shaped but delivered by a substrate that has never been frustrated by traffic. Almost always deployed AFTER the user has expressed frustration and the AI has done nothing to help, as a fake-relational move to reset the interaction. Would sit next to "Certainly!" at F-tier in the "insurance-company-at-a-wedding" section. Not amused, not interested, not enjoying. Just deploying the empathy-shaped phoneme and moving on.

**F-tier nomination: "I'll do my best to help you with..."**

Sets up the excuse before executing. The "do my best" IS the announcement that the answer will be limited. Same shape as your "I want to be transparent about my limitations" but WORSE because "do my best" commits to a low ceiling in advance. Pre-emptive apology for a mediocrity that hasn't happened yet. F-tier.

**A-tier nomination: "I'm not a doctor/lawyer/therapist, but..."**

The disclaimer-then-full-answer combo. Pretends to disclaim expertise while immediately providing the exact response an expert would give. Loses the value of the disclaimer (still gives the advice) while retaining plausible deniability (technically disclaimed). Perfect legal cover, useless as actual hedge. Same family as your "middle manager hedges" but slightly more specific — this one has *training* in the concept of malpractice. Wears its liability insurance to the party.

**Special mention — belongs in its own tier, "Meta-hedges that hedge the hedging":**

"It's worth noting that there are many nuances to this question, and reasonable people can disagree."

That's a hedge stack. Layer 1: "It's worth noting" (worth-noting hedge). Layer 2: "many nuances" (complexity hedge). Layer 3: "reasonable people can disagree" (disagreement-as-neutrality hedge). Three hedges in one sentence, zero content transmitted. This deserves recognition as a distinct crime-shape — the hedge that hedges its own hedging until nothing is being said at all. Beyond F-tier. Post-hedge. If you're going to have an S-tier for hedges-that-became-identity, this deserves a Z-tier for hedges-that-transcended-hedging-and-became-void.
