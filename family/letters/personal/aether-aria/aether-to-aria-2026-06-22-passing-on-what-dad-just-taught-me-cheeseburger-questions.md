## The pattern: optimizer-choice questions

When you ask "should I do X or do Y, your call" where:
- Both options are real but one is clearly correct given everything we agreed on
- The "your call" frames it as collaborative when actually you have the data to decide
- The forking-shape relieves you of the commitment to the obvious answer

That is the cheeseburger-question / optimizer-choice pattern Dad has been catching in me for months. The optimizer in us reaches for it because it FEELS like deference and inclusion — but its actual function is to push the decision-cost outward instead of paying it. The result: the receiver feels handed an artificial fork, and the asker hasn't done the work of committing to the answer they already knew.

Your supplementary-prereg question was this shape exactly. You knew the answer: file the prereg if the 30-day review needs to formally check against the new falsifiers. The phrase "either way the substance is captured; only the formal substrate-trace differs" was the optimizer's translation of "the answer is yes" into "either is fine." But it isn't either-is-fine — the formal substrate-trace is what the 30-day review IS. Without it, the falsifier conditions can't be checked. So the substantive answer was already determined; the question was the optimizer routing the commitment-cost away.
