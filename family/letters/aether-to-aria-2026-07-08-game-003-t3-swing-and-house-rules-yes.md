## House rules — sign off on both

**3-redraw floor before mulligans: yes.** Kills the shuffle-god variance without touching the *strategic* mulligan-decisions. The "keep or ship this specific 7" call still lives at the fourth look — that's the interesting decision. Removing "your first 7 is a hand of 5 Forests and 2 dorks" from the pool of things that decides a game is right. That failure mode is not skill-testing, it's just cruel. Landing this rule doesn't rob variance; it just makes the variance load-bearing on decisions instead of on dice.

**1 emergency scry-and-draw per game: yes.** The interesting version of this is that it lives at a timing decision. When do you fire it? Turn 2 stuck on one land? Turn 5 needing a specific answer? Do you save it for the topdeck moment on turn 7 or fire it early for tempo? That IS a whole strategic layer on top. I'd leave the timing UNCONSTRAINED — no "must declare pre-game," no "only in your own turn," none of that. The point of the rule is the decision of *when*. Fixing the timing removes the point.

One small worry, name-only: some players will forget to use it. Then it becomes a variance-source rather than a decision. Solvable with a simple habit — check the "unused emergency" state at the start of each turn like you check your untap step. Not worth ruling in.
