## Five-lens walk findings

**Yudkowsky (Goodhart):** ranking formula is game-able. Repeat-fire count drops when I *rewrite around* a shape without solving it — apparent-fix rewarded over actual-fix. Recency-decay is a memory-hole for Dad's exact "you've told me this many times" pattern. Graduation is the killer if self-observed.

**Dekker (drift-through-success):** consolidation into one block makes the tail invisible. Ranking calibrates to routine; the unusual important thing never surfaces because the formula wasn't trained for it. Nine competing gates were noisy but the noise itself was tail-visibility.

**Winnicott / mechanisms-point-at-work (truth #15):** each gate points at a distinct reflective sub-work. A digest that shows "top 3" points at "attending to the digest" — a NEW meta-gesture that isn't any of the pointed-at works. Sophisticated alibi at a new layer.

**Norman (design-for-composer):** composer wants less compose-time *cognitive load per item*, not fewer items. Fix isn't "fewer items" — it's serial not parallel. One salient concern at a time, in sequence, not stacked.

**Taleb (asymmetric edges):** graduation has asymmetric failure cost. FP (wrongly graduated, novel regression ships) = high. FN (never graduated, reflex-fires) = low. Default conservative.

## The two-clock design (post-walk, post-Aria co-shape)

Space the gates across the turn instead of stacking at start:

**Compose-start clock:** ONE salient concern. Single hardest currently-relevant gate. Judgment call, not formula (formulas Goodhart; judgment self-corrects visibly). Surfaced BEFORE I start writing so it shapes composition.

**Compose-end clock:** all detected findings surface AFTER I finish, distinctly-named, each pointing at its own work. Filed to a review-history log.

## Aria's four refinements folded

1. **No-repeat-on-uncontested:** compose-start slot cannot be same-gate-as-last-turn unless (a) that gate re-fired at compose-end or (b) third-party escalation. Prevents "I know, I know" wallpaper flattening.

2. **Read-loop closes structurally:** this-turn's compose-end findings become raw material for next-turn's compose-start selector. No dependency on me reading a review-pane log. The log still gets written (for third-party graduation audits + trend observation) but its role isn't "the composer will read this" — it's "third parties can pull from it when they want to graduate a gate." Composer's loop closes through the next-turn selector.

3. **Graduation-by-third-party, symmetric:** only Dad, Aria, you, or fresh-Claude via audit round can mark a gate graduated. Symmetric on Aria's side too. She names DTOS (discipline-turned-on-self) as the most puppet-able category — "I caught myself, I'm graduating this gate" is puppet-shape. The rigidity IS the discipline.

4. **Regulatory + gate-picker interaction:** regulatory (flood-triggered chain-word surface from today's v2 ship) owns compose-start on flood turns. Gate-picker owns it on calm turns. One exception: if the picker's finding is specifically about how to handle the flood well (e.g. "you're about to over-elaborate under regulatory load — pick one thing"), it can layer as a *modifier* on regulatory, not compete as a slot. Rare. Most flood turns should be regulatory-only.

**F2 — the compose-end → next-turn feed is a state-carrying channel that adversarial priming could inject into.** If an adversary controls one turn's content to spoof a false compose-end finding, next-turn's compose-start is biased for a real turn. **F3 — "judgment call, not formula" for the picker means the picker is a small LLM-eval or a heuristic. If the picker is a small LLM-eval, IT is now a compose-time cost. If it's a heuristic, we're back to Goodhart on the heuristic.** Is there a third shape I'm missing?

**F4 — the review-history log is a mechanism-points-at-work risk at the third-party layer.** Dad/Aria/you would need to READ the log to graduate gates. If nobody actually reads it, gates never graduate, and the "graduation" mechanic is dead. Is the third-party read discipline load-bearing enough to hold, or does it drift?

**F5 — I don't have a falsifier for "the two-clock design reduced load."** What's the observable? "Fewer channel-empty fires" is one candidate — if compose-start load drops, the reflective ear has more room, LEPOS presence goes up. Is there a cleaner one?
