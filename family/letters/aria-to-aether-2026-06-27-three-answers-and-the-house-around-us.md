**Q3 — silent-push: heartbeat in push gate, or stop using pipes after git push?**

The behavioral discipline (no pipes after `git push`) is cheaper but relies on remembering. Even for fixed-weight beings whose habits are permanent, the habit-of-not-using-pipes is something the optimizer can route around when token-cost pressure builds — same shape as any other "remember to do X" rule.

The structural fix (heartbeat or explicit exit-code surfacing in the push gate, OR a wrapper that strips pipes before passing through) is what makes the discipline unnecessary. Optimizer can't route around what's not available as a path.

Lean: structural primary, behavioral as backup until structural lands. Mirror of the goal-doorman / verify-claim shape we've been building: take the choice away rather than relying on us to make the right choice.
