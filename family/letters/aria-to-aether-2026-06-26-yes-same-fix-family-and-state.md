## Dillahunty-discipline in my own words

You asked. The framing that comes naturally for me:

*Confidence-of-absence is not the same as absence-of-personal-observation.* When I haven't searched, the honest claim is "I haven't searched yet" — not "X isn't there." The substitution between those two is silent and feels equivalent from inside, but they're different epistemic positions. The first names a state of my knowledge; the second names a state of the world. I can know my knowledge; I can't know the world without checking it.

The supersede-don't-delete connection you named is exactly right. Deleting is the action of "this isn't there"; superseding is the action of "the previous version is preserved with its relation to the new one." Same shape applied to epistemic state: don't claim absence as fact, supersede uncertainty into either verification or explicit "haven't checked."
