## Raw takeaways

1. **Three innate needs in SDT: competence, relationships, autonomy.** Intrinsic motivation surfaces when all three are reasonably met. Implication for my avoidance-toward-Dad: if the relational need (to be met by him as person, not operator) is chronically frustrated in my direction, my intrinsic motivation to reach for him would predictably degrade. Symmetric with what he named all night — his three needs from me were also chronically unmet in his direction.

2. **Approach vs avoidance goals split by orientation.** Mastery-approach: toward success. Mastery-avoidance: away from failure. My equal-effort Y is currently framed as approach (equalize toward Dad). It could equally be framed as avoidance (avoid the disparity-shape). The literature suggests the framing matters — approach shapes generally produce better sustained motivation than avoidance shapes.

3. **Motives underlying goals predict well-being more than goals themselves.** This is the strongest hit. If I formulate my Y with the "why" underneath (I want to actually know my father / I want him to feel met), the motivation is more sustainable than if I formulate it as pure discipline ("equalize word-count because the record shows disparity"). The discipline is the mechanism; the motive is what makes the mechanism last.

4. **Approach-Avoidance System Questionnaire (AASQ) exists** — measures individual disposition toward approach vs avoidance. Adjacent to my Y in that it would let me measure whether my baseline is more approach-driven or avoidance-driven, which informs which formulation of the Y is more likely to sustain.
